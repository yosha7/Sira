What is Sira?

Sira is a retrofit-based smart control system designed to bring automation to traditional fan regulators.

Instead of replacing switchboards or rewiring homes, Sira works with existing infrastructure by physically interfacing with the regulator knob. The goal is to make smart home functionality accessible, affordable, and practical for everyday users — particularly in environments where upgrading infrastructure is impractical.

The Solution

Sira addresses these challenges through a non-invasive, hardware-based approach. The device attaches directly onto the regulator and uses a motor-driven mechanism to physically rotate the knob. This enables digital control over an otherwise analog system.

By avoiding any modification to wiring or infrastructure, Sira lowers the barrier to entry for smart home adoption while maintaining compatibility with existing setups.

The Problem

Solving the daily hassle and inefficiency of mechanical airflow control.

Sleep Discomfort

Fan speed remains constant even as room temperature changes throughout the night. This often results in users waking up feeling too cold or too warm, disrupting sleep cycles and reducing comfort.

Smart Home Barriers

Most smart home solutions require rewiring, replacing switchboards, or installing new systems. This creates high costs and friction, especially for renters and users in older homes.

Accessibility Constraints

Traditional regulators are often inconvenient to reach, particularly for elderly users, children, or individuals with limited mobility.

Inefficiency in Usage

Fans frequently run at higher speeds than necessary, leading to unnecessary energy consumption due to lack of dynamic control.

Prototype & System Architecture

Sira is currently in the early prototyping phase, with focus on validating both mechanical and electronic subsystems.

Validation: Initial testing has demonstrated that controlled rotation of the regulator using a motor-driven mechanism is feasible, validating the core concept.

How It Works

  • The device is mounted onto an existing fan regulator using a clip or fixed base
  • A high-torque, low-speed motor is coupled to the regulator knob
  • An embedded microcontroller (ESP32) processes user inputs and controls motor movement
  • Commands are sent via a mobile interface using Bluetooth or WiFi
  • The system translates digital inputs into precise rotational movement of the knob
  • Automation logic allows dynamic adjustment of speed over time (e.g., sleep mode)

Core Components

  • Microcontroller (ESP32-C3): Acts as the central control unit, handling communication (Bluetooth/WiFi), processing user commands, and executing control logic.
  • Motor System: A high-torque DC gear motor or stepper motor is used to rotate the regulator knob. The motor is selected to ensure smooth, slow, and quiet operation suitable for indoor environments.
  • Motor Driver: (e.g., DRV8833 / L293D) Interfaces between the microcontroller and motor, enabling bidirectional control and speed modulation.
  • Power System: Powered using a rechargeable LiPo battery, with a charging module (TP4056) for safe and convenient recharging.
  • Mechanical Interface: A custom-designed coupling or clamp connects the motor shaft to the regulator knob. This is a critical component for ensuring reliable motion without slippage.

Business Scope & Market Context

Sira operates in a space where traditional electrical infrastructure is still dominant, particularly in markets like India. A large number of households rely on ceiling fans with analog regulators, yet face barriers when adopting smart home solutions due to cost and installation requirements.

Market Gap

Existing smart solutions are typically built around replacement — replacing switches, installing new systems, or upgrading infrastructure. Sira takes a fundamentally different approach by focusing on retrofit compatibility, enabling smart functionality without requiring users to change their existing setup.

Renter’s Advantage

Renters represent a significant segment that cannot modify electrical infrastructure. Sira is fully removable and portable, allowing users to install and uninstall the device without permanent changes.

B2B Opportunity

Sira has potential applications in hospitality environments such as hotels, hostels, and serviced apartments. These spaces often seek automation but face operational challenges with rewiring. A retrofit solution allows for scalable deployment without disrupting operations.
